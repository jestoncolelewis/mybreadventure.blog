The White Loaf of Toast Glory

This is the most basic of loaves. The only thing that sets this a part from the most ancient of dough (sourdough) is the oil and time. This consisted of the following:

  • 500g of Bread Flour
  • 9g of Active Dry Yeast
  • 10g of Salt
  • 60ml of Avocado Oil
  • 270ml of Water

I love the fact that I don't actually have to activate this yeast. Just throwing it in with everything gets the process started. Unlike my other experiments, this dough likes being a singular entity. The chicken aide is a beast when it comes to joining all of these ingredients together.

The white loaf dough is also a stickier dough than my previous loaves. The bread flour really produces that great gluten structure right off the bat and only needs a little bit of kneading after coming out of the mixer.

The first proof was a full hour. I find it interesting how the white loaf continues to smell like flour throughout the proofing process whereas the other loaves start smelling like their final the final loaf a lot earlier in the process.

I did the standard transition that I've been experimenting with from the first to the second proof. As you can see, I squish the loaf down into the tin after removing as much air from the first proof as feels right. I feel like this might be developing the flavor more but who am I kidding, that's just something I've heard or read about flavor.

The second proof was just over of an hour at one and fifteen before I popped it in the oven at 425ºF for 33 minutes. I've found that my over at 425ºF needs a little longer than I would expect for a loaf like this (my expectations are closer to 30 minutes).

Then came the sadness. In my eagerness to release the loaf from its tin, I cracked the crust. Luckily, the loaf was still pretty warm so I just lumped the broken bit onto the end and it was all good.

Overall, this a great toast or sandwich loaf. The broken corner was sad but didn't effect the taste in the slightest. It did make the first several slices a bit of a pain to cut, but that's the price I pay for being hasty. I've definitely learned my lesson on that front.

Next week, I'm continuing with my exploration of tin loaves. Maybe soon I'll do something different but no promises.
